## First Aid Guide: Fever

**1. Definition and Symptoms**

*   **Definition:** Fever is defined as an elevation in body temperature above the normal range. The normal body temperature varies depending on the individual, time of day, activity level, and measurement method. Generally, the following thresholds are considered indicative of a fever:
    *   **Oral:** 100°F (37.8°C) or higher
    *   **Rectal:** 100.4°F (38°C) or higher
    *   **Axillary (armpit):** 99°F (37.2°C) or higher
    *   **Tympanic (ear):** 100.4°F (38°C) or higher

*   **Symptoms:** Fever is often accompanied by a range of symptoms, including:
    *   Chills and shivering
    *   Sweating
    *   Headache
    *   Muscle aches
    *   Weakness and fatigue
    *   Loss of appetite
    *   Dehydration
    *   Flushed skin
    *   Irritability
    *   Confusion or disorientation (in severe cases)
    *   Febrile seizures (in infants and young children)

**2. When to Seek Emergency Medical Help**

Immediate medical attention is required if a fever is accompanied by any of the following:

*   **Infants under 3 months (12 weeks) with a rectal temperature of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher.**
*   **High fever:**
    *   Temperature of 104°F (40°C) or higher in adults and older children.
*   **Severe symptoms:**
    *   Stiff neck
    *   Severe headache
    *   Seizures
    *   Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
    *   Chest pain
    *   Abdominal pain
    *   Persistent vomiting or diarrhea
    *   Rash (especially if it doesn't blanch when pressed)
    *   Confusion or disorientation
    *   Lethargy or unresponsiveness
    *   Dehydration (signs include decreased urination, dry mouth, dizziness)
    *   Pain when urinating
    *   Recent surgery or immunocompromised state (e.g., chemotherapy, HIV/AIDS)
*   **Symptoms persist or worsen:**
    *   Fever lasting longer than 3 days in adults.
    *   Fever lasting longer than 24 hours in infants and children.

**3. Step-by-Step First Aid Procedures**

1.  **Assess the Situation:**
    *   Determine the individual's level of consciousness and breathing.
    *   Check for any other symptoms besides fever.
2.  **Measure Body Temperature:**
    *   Use a reliable thermometer to accurately measure the body temperature using the appropriate method (oral, rectal, axillary, tympanic).
    *   Record the temperature and the method of measurement.
3.  **Comfort Measures:**
    *   **Cooling:**
        *   Apply lukewarm compresses (not ice-cold) to the forehead, armpits, and groin.
        *   Offer a lukewarm bath or sponge bath (avoid cold water, which can cause shivering and further increase body temperature).
        *   Ensure the room is well-ventilated and maintain a comfortable temperature.
    *   **Hydration:**
        *   Encourage the individual to drink plenty of fluids (water, clear broth, diluted juice, electrolyte solutions). Avoid sugary drinks.
    *   **Rest:**
        *   Promote rest and minimize activity to conserve energy.
4.  **Medication (if appropriate):**
    *   **Over-the-counter (OTC) medications:**
        *   **Acetaminophen (Tylenol, Panadol):** Follow dosage instructions carefully, especially for children, based on weight. Do not exceed the recommended daily dose.
        *   **Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin):** Follow dosage instructions carefully, especially for children, based on weight. Do not exceed the recommended daily dose.  Not recommended for infants under 6 months without consulting a doctor.
        *   **Aspirin:** **Do not give aspirin to children or teenagers due to the risk of Reye's syndrome.**
    *   **Important Considerations:**
        *   Do not alternate between acetaminophen and ibuprofen unless advised by a healthcare professional.
        *   Read and follow all medication labels carefully.
        *   Do not give cold and flu medications to children under 6 years old unless specifically instructed by a doctor.
5.  **Monitor the Individual:**
    *   Check the temperature periodically (every 1-2 hours initially) to assess the effectiveness of the interventions.
    *   Observe for any changes in symptoms or the development of new symptoms.
6.  **Provide Information to Medical Professionals:**
    *   If seeking medical attention, be prepared to provide information on:
        *   Temperature readings and when they were taken.
        *   Any other symptoms present.
        *   Medications taken and dosages.
        *   Underlying medical conditions.
        *   Recent travel history.

**4. Home Remedies and Self-Care Tips**

*   **Rest:** Adequate rest is crucial for the body to fight infection.
*   **Hydration:** Drink plenty of fluids to prevent dehydration.
*   **Nutrition:** Eat light, easily digestible foods such as soup or crackers.
*   **Avoid Overexertion:** Limit physical activity and avoid strenuous tasks.
*   **Loose Clothing:** Wear light, breathable clothing to allow for better heat dissipation.
*   **Humidifier:** Use a humidifier to add moisture to the air, which can help relieve congestion and soothe a sore throat.
*   **Honey (for cough):** For children over 1 year of age, honey can help soothe a cough. Give 1-2 teaspoons as needed.
*   **Avoid alcohol and tobacco:** These substances can worsen symptoms and impair the immune system.

**5. Prevention Measures**

*   **Good Hygiene:**
    *   Wash hands frequently with soap and water, especially after coughing, sneezing, or touching surfaces in public places.
    *   Use alcohol-based hand sanitizer if soap and water are not available.
*   **Avoid Contact with Sick Individuals:**
    *   Limit close contact with people who are sick.
    *   Avoid sharing utensils, cups, and other personal items.
*   **Vaccination:**
    *   Stay up-to-date with recommended vaccinations, including the influenza (flu) vaccine and other vaccines that protect against infectious diseases.
*   **Healthy Lifestyle:**
    *   Maintain a healthy diet, get enough sleep, and engage in regular physical activity to strengthen the immune system.
*   **Respiratory Etiquette:**
    *   Cover your mouth and nose with a tissue when coughing or sneezing. If a tissue is not available, cough or sneeze into your elbow.
    *   Dispose of used tissues properly.
*   **Clean and Disinfect Surfaces:**
    *   Regularly clean and disinfect frequently touched surfaces, such as doorknobs, countertops, and light switches, especially during outbreaks of infectious diseases.
